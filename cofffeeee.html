Our History

Origins: Coffee is believed to have originated in the region of Ethiopia, in the area known as Kaffa. Legend has it that a goat herder named Kaldi discovered coffee when he noticed his goats becoming lively after eating berries from a certain plant. He tried the berries himself and experienced increased energy.

Coffee cultivation and consumption spread from Ethiopia to the Arabian Peninsula, particularly Yemen, by the 15th century. Yemeni traders began cultivating coffee in their own region, and it quickly became popular in Islamic culture. Coffee houses, called qahveh khaneh, started to emerge in cities like Constantinople (modern-day Istanbul).

Coffeehouses became centers for socializing, intellectual discussion, and even business transactions. Coffee's popularity spread throughout the Ottoman Empire and into Persia, North Africa, and the rest of the Middle East.
